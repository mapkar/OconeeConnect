OconeeConnect Connection Instructions

Join the local mesh network in Oconee County, SC—easy setup with default settings!

Quick Overview

OconeeConnect uses Meshtastic's default settings for simplicity: the primary channel is "LongFast" with default encryption. This means your device can connect out-of-the-box without custom configs. Just get in range of other nodes, and you're part of the mesh!

Default Settings We're Using:
  • Channel: Primary (Channel 0), named "LongFast".
  • Preset: Long Fast (good balance of range and speed—up to several miles line-of-sight).
  • Encryption: Default PSK ("AQ==")—it's basic encryption with a public key, so anyone on defaults can join. (For private chats, use secondary channels or DMs.)
  • Region: US (915 MHz band)—auto-detected based on your location.

It's great for off-grid texting and location sharing in Oconee County's towns, lakes, mountains, and rural areas.

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Get Hardware: Buy a Meshtastic-compatible LoRa device (e.g., Heltec V3, LilyGO T-Beam, RAK WisBlock—$20–$50 on Amazon, AliExpress, or stores like Rokland). Many come pre-flashed with Meshtastic.
  2. Install Firmware (if needed):
    • Go to flasher.meshtastic.org in a web browser (Chrome/Edge).
    • Connect your device via USB.
    • Select your device model, choose the latest firmware (defaults are fine), and flash it.
  3. Download the App:
    • Android: Search "Meshtastic" on Google Play.
    • iOS: Search "Meshtastic" on App Store.
    • Web/Desktop: Use client.meshtastic.org.
  4. Pair Your Device:
    • Turn on your LoRa device (it should boot into Meshtastic mode).
    • Open the app, enable Bluetooth, and scan for devices.
    • Select your device—it'll connect automatically.
  5. Connect to the Mesh:
    • No changes needed! Your device is already on the default "LongFast" channel.
    • Go to the app's "Mesh" or "Channels" tab—you should see nearby nodes if any are active.
    • Send a test message to "All" or a specific node ID.
  6. Join OconeeConnect Locally:
    • Get in range of an existing node in Oconee County, SC (we're building coverage in key areas—check our repo for node locations or updates).
    • Key coverage targets include: Seneca, Walhalla, Westminster, Salem, West Union, Long Creek, Mountain Rest, Tamassee, Fair Play, Keowee Key, and surrounding lake/mountain communities.
    • If you see nodes but no connection, double-check your region is set to "US" in Device Settings > LoRa (it auto-sets usually).
    • Troubleshoot: Restart device/app, ensure antenna is attached, and try outdoors/elevated spots for better range (especially useful in the foothills and near Lakes Keowee/Hartwell).

That's it! Your messages will hop through the mesh automatically. For advanced tips, like adding solar-powered nodes or private channels, see the Meshtastic docs at meshtastic.org.

Tips & Safety

  • Range varies: 1–5 miles in towns/lakeside, 10+ miles in open/rural mountain areas.
  • Battery life: Days on default settings—use power-saving modes for longer (great for hiking in Sumter National Forest).
  • Legal: No license needed in US for LoRa on ISM bands, but keep power low.
  • Privacy: Defaults are public—use DMs for sensitive info.
  • Contribute: Set up a node in your area (e.g., Seneca, Walhalla, or a lakeside spot) and share your location on our GitHub issues!
